# Clarifying questions — brainstorm stage

> Asked by the operator (or `strategist` chatmode) after reading the prospect's `proposal.md`. Each question has a *why* — share it if the prospect resists. Goal: produce enough signal to write `02-plan/plan.md` in one sitting.

---

## Block A — Sharpen the audience (must answer before plan)

### Q1. Of the people in your target audience, name 5 you could DM today.
> *Why:* If you can't name 5, the audience is theoretical. We need to define it tighter or pivot.

### Q2. What did the last 3 of those 5 buy in the last 12 months that's adjacent to what you're selling?
> *Why:* Willingness-to-pay signal. If no one in your "audience" has paid for anything similar, the category is unproven for them.

### Q3. Where do they complain about the problem you're solving? Show me 3 links.
> *Why:* If you can't find public complaints, the pain isn't acute enough to drive purchase.

---

## Block B — Pressure-test the offering

### Q4. Pick ONE of your offerings from §7 of the proposal. What does the buyer hold in their hand 7 days after paying?
> *Why:* Vague offerings don't sell. Force a concrete deliverable.

### Q5. What's the cheapest version of that offering you could ship in 2 weeks?
> *Why:* MVP entry. Whatever you say here becomes your A1.

### Q6. What's the equivalent of "Fractional CTO retainer" for your space? Who pays $2K+/mo and what do they expect?
> *Why:* Anchors high-ticket Tier C. If there's no analog, the OS will be cohort-heavy not retainer-heavy — different cashflow shape.

---

## Block C — Time & energy reality

### Q7. Pick a specific week in the next 8. Walk me through the 7 hours you'd give this — which days, what time of day, around what existing commitments?
> *Why:* "7 hours" sounds easy until you map it onto a real calendar. This question kills 1 in 3 plans.

### Q8. What's the FIRST thing you'd skip when life gets busy? Sales, content, delivery, or ops?
> *Why:* Whatever they say will be skipped → the OS guardrails must protect it.

### Q9. When was the last time you sustained a side project for 6+ months? What killed it (or how did you keep it alive)?
> *Why:* Predicts dropout risk. Adjusts realistic timeline and milestone spacing.

---

## Block D — Money discipline

### Q10. What's the SMALLEST monthly revenue that would make you proud (not "successful" — proud)?
> *Why:* This is the milestone we'll celebrate hardest. Often 1/3 of what they originally said.

### Q11. What's the LARGEST single check you've collected from a paying customer (in any context)? What did they get?
> *Why:* Calibrates their internal anchor for "what high-ticket means." Sets realistic C1/C3 pricing.

### Q12. Are there any deals (current employer, ex-colleagues, friends-and-family) you should NOT pursue, even if offered?
> *Why:* Conflict-of-interest firewall. Goes straight into `do-not-contact.md` in the generated OS.

---

## Optional Block E — for D2 Founder-in-Residence engagements only

### Q13. Who are the 3 people whose opinion of this venture you most care about?
### Q14. If this fails in 18 months, what's the story you'd tell yourself about why?
### Q15. What's a decision you've already made about this that you're NOT willing to relitigate?

---

## After the call

1. Operator commits answers to `01-brainstorm/transcript.md` (verbatim or summarized — note which).
2. Run `/red-team` on the proposal + answers — surface contradictions.
3. If ≥3 questions are unanswered or contradicted → schedule a second call before writing the plan.
4. If clean → proceed to `02-plan/plan-template.md`.

## Hard rules for the interviewer

- Don't pitch any solutions during the call. You're listening, not selling.
- Don't fill silences. Wait 5 seconds after every answer.
- Numbers > adjectives. Push back on "many" / "most" / "growing."
- One question at a time. Never batch.
- Capture the exact phrasing they use for their problem — those become content hooks.
