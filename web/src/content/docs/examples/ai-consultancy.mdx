## The operator

**Jane Doe** is a senior data scientist at MegaCorp. She's building an AI consulting practice on evenings and weekends, ~10 hours/week. Goals:

- Land 2 small AI-strategy engagements in 90 days
- Build a credible LinkedIn/newsletter presence in her voice
- Never compromise her day job (firewall is critical)
- Get to $5k/mo retained income before quitting

This is the **flagship use case** for founderstaff.

## Pack selection

```yaml
# .platform/skills.manifest.yaml
upstream: https://github.com/themeinnov8/founderstaff.git
branch: main
packs:
  - core
  - research
  - content
  - sales
  - delivery
  - compliance     # critical — day-job firewall
  - finance
```

Why not `marketing` or `product`? At this stage, Jane is consulting (services), not building (product), and her marketing is primarily content + outreach — `content` and `sales` cover it.

## A week in the life

### Monday morning (45 min)

```
> /run-due
```

Output:

```
DUE TODAY:
  [1] chief-of-staff-daily        → /next-best-action
  [2] inbox-triage-weekdays       → /inbox-triage
  [3] linkedin-daily              → /linkedin-post
  [4] research-weekly             → /market-research
  [5] pipeline-monthly (council)  → /council monthly-pipeline-review

OVERDUE:
  [6] delivery-weekly             → /weekly-status (skipped Friday)

Run all in order? (y/n/select):
```

Jane picks `select`, runs `[1] [2] [6]` now (45 min), defers the rest to evening.

**Chief of staff** returns:

> Today's one next action: Send the proposal to ProspectX (drafted Friday, sitting in `04-sales/proposals/prospectx-2026-05-24.md`). Reasoning: highest-value open deal, oldest pending action (4 days), prospect signaled urgency. Estimated 15 min including compliance check.

She sends the proposal. Then `/inbox-triage` produces 3 draft replies; she ships 2, archives 8 emails. `/weekly-status` reconciles Friday and surfaces an overdue update to ClientA — she sends that too.

### Tuesday evening (60 min)

```
> @research-analyst
> /market-research what are mid-market companies saying about LLM-cost vs LLM-quality tradeoffs in 2026 Q2?
```

Returns cited research note in `02-research/llm-cost-quality-2026q2.md` with 8 sources, 3 confidence-MEDIUM claims flagged, 2 unknowns.

Jane then:

```
> @content-creator
> Read 02-research/llm-cost-quality-2026q2.md. Plan one blog and three LinkedIn posts.
```

`@content-creator` produces a content brief (doesn't write yet) in `03-content/briefs/llm-cost-quality.md`.

### Wednesday morning (30 min)

```
> /run-due
> /linkedin-post topic="why mid-market is rebudgeting LLM spend in 2026Q2"
```

`linkedin-ghostwriter` drafts the post. `/compliance-check` runs automatically — flags one phrase that's too close to MegaCorp internal terminology. Jane edits, re-runs, passes. Posts.

### Thursday — client work (3 hours)

Outside the OS — actual delivery work. Time-log entry added.

### Friday afternoon (30 min)

```
> /weekly-status all clients
```

Returns 3 status entries (one per active client), each reconciled to the time-log, sent as separate emails.

```
> @sales-coach
> /discovery-call-prep prospect="LeadCo" call="Mon 10am"
```

One-page brief in `04-sales/calls/leadco-prep.md`.

### Saturday morning (45 min) — weekly review

```
> /weekly-review
```

`operations-manager` reads `09-meta/time-log.md`, `daily-log.md`, `decisions/`, `rhythms/runs.md`. Returns:

- Hours by category (delivery 4h, sales 2h, content 1.5h, admin 1h, learning 1.5h = 10h)
- Plan deltas (planned to ship 2 blog posts, shipped 1)
- Carry-forward (research note → blog draft for next week)
- Stop-doing recommendation (one rhythm hasn't produced value in 4 weeks — propose retire)

She accepts the retire recommendation, runs `/retire-agent <id>` for the unused agent.

### Sunday morning (60 min) — content batch

```
> @content-creator
> /newsletter-issue weekly digest
```

Produces full issue from the week's notes + research. `/compliance-check` passes. Jane edits headline, schedules to send Tuesday.

```
> /linkedin-carousel topic="LLM cost-quality tradeoff (from this week's research)"
```

5-slide carousel; she ships Monday.

### Monthly (first weekend of the month)

```
> /run-due
[1] cashflow-monthly  → /cashflow-forecast
[2] pipeline-council  → /council monthly-pipeline-review (3 agents)
```

She runs `[1]` solo. For `[2]`, she invokes — 5 phases unfold, full transcript in `09-meta/rhythms/councils/2026-06-02-pipeline-monthly.md`. Synthesis: 1 decision (kill stalled-prospect outreach), 3 action items, 1 preserved disagreement (sales-coach wants tighter ICP; delivery-lead wants broader). Jane logs the disagreement as an ADR draft for the quarterly council.

## Failure modes

| Symptom | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| `/compliance-check` blocking benign drafts | Tokens too aggressive in firewall config | Refine `01-strategy/compliance.md` to scope EMPLOYER terminology more precisely |
| Rhythm queue growing unbounded | Operator stopped running `/run-due` daily | Re-establish morning trigger; consider reducing cadence on lowest-value rhythms |
| Council producing fake consensus | Coordinator instructions not enforcing "preserve disagreement" | Re-sync from upstream (`relearn.py`) — likely a stale `councils.md` |
| `@chief-of-staff` returning ambiguous "I see 4 things you could do" | Agent has access to too much; declared scope drifted | Read the chatmode file; tighten its `tools` + write-scope; re-instantiate |

## What this OS produces in 90 days

Realistic targets (based on the 10h/week budget):

- **Content**: 12 newsletter issues, ~60 LinkedIn posts, 6 blog posts, 8 carousels
- **Sales**: ~80 outbound DMs, ~40 discovery calls scheduled, 2 closed engagements
- **Delivery**: 1–2 concurrent clients, weekly status reconciled, 1 case study
- **Compliance**: zero firewall breaches (the system enforces this)
- **Self-evolution**: 2–4 new skills forged through `@hiring-manager`, contributed back upstream

Jane crosses $5k/mo MRR around day 75, gives notice day 120.
