## Why this exists

Most founders fail at **execution**, not ideas. They lose to:

- **Decision fatigue** — too many small choices, not enough cognitive budget.
- **Scattered state** — plan in Notion, leads in a spreadsheet, content in drafts, decisions in their head.
- **Solo blind spots** — no chief of staff, no red-teamer, no research analyst.
- **Compliance drift** — especially when running a venture alongside a day job.

**founderstaff turns your editor into a roster of AI co-workers**, all reading the same workspace, all guardrailed, all on schedule.

## Design principles

1. **Time-first, revenue-second.** Default config assumes 5–10 hours/week.
2. **Cheap markdown, rich HTML.** State in MD. Visualizations regenerated on demand.
3. **Scoped agents.** Every chatmode declares read/write scope. No unrestricted access.
4. **Compliance baseline.** Day-job firewall, citation discipline, decisions logged.
5. **Never invent.** Research guardrails refuse fabrication.
6. **One next action.** `@chief-of-staff` returns ONE action with reasoning, never a buffet.
7. **Self-evolution as code.** New skills check in as PRs.
